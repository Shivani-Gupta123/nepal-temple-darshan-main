Janki Kund in Chitrakoot-Dham

Janki Kund is a sacred water tank in the Chitrakoot district of Uttar Pradesh, India. It is considered an important pilgrimage site for Hindus, who believe that a dip in the tank can purify one's soul and bring blessings from Lord Rama and his wife, Sita. According to legend, it was at Janki Kund that Sita used to bathe during her stay in Chitrakoot.

Location of Chitrakoot Dham

Chitrakoot is situated about 300 kilometers from Gorakhpur, he tank is surrounded by beautiful stone steps and temples, including the Janki Temple dedicated to Sita. Visitors come to Janki Kund to offer prayers, dip in the holy water, and seek blessings from Lord Rama and Sita. The site is especially popular during festivals such as Diwali and Ram Navami, when thousands of devotees gather to offer prayers and participate in various rituals.

Ram Ghat in Chitrakoot

Ram Ghat is a popular tourist destination in Chitrakoot, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is a famous pilgrimage site for Hindus and is situated on the banks of the river Mandakini. Ram Ghat is known for its religious significance and is believed to be where Lord Rama and his wife Sita bathed during their exile. It is also thought to be where Tulsidas, the famous Hindi poet, wrote the Ramcharitmanas.

Location of Chitrakoot Dham

Tourists can take a holy dip in the river Mandakini at Ram Ghat, which is considered a purifying experience. The ghat is lined with numerous temples decorated beautifully with colorful lights and flowers during festivals. Besides the religious significance, Ram Ghat offers a picturesque view of the surrounding hills and is a popular spot for photographers. Visitors can also enjoy a boat ride on the river Mandakini and witness the serene beauty of the place.

Sati Anusuya Ashrama in Chitrakoot

Sati Anusuya Ashrama is an important pilgrimage site in Chitrakoot, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is believed to be where the revered sage Atri Muni and his wife, Sati Anusuya, meditated and performed penance. The ashram has a small temple dedicated to Sati Anusuya and a sacred pond known as Sati Anusuya Kund, which is believed to have miraculous powers. Devotees come from far and wide to dip in the holy pond and seek blessings from the goddess. The ashram is surrounded by lush green forests and is a peaceful place to meditate and connect with nature. It is a must-visit for those seeking spiritual solace and looking to experience the divine energy of the place.
